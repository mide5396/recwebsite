What is Canned Turkey?

Canned turkey is simply turkey meat that has been cooked, shredded, and preserved in a can, often along with broth, for easy preparation. It can be made from both white and dark meat and is usually packed in its own juices or with added liquid like broth or gravy for extra flavor.

The History of Turkey as Food

Turkeys have been a food source for centuries, dating back to the indigenous peoples of North and Central America. Domesticated by the Aztecs and other early civilizations, turkeys were later introduced to Europe in the 16th century. They quickly became a popular dish due to their size and rich flavor.

By the 19th century, turkey had solidified its place as a staple in holiday meals, particularly in the United States and Canada. The tradition of eating turkey on Thanksgiving is said to date back to the Pilgrims' first feast in 1621, though historians debate whether turkey was actually served.

Why is Turkey So Popular?

Turkey is a versatile meat that can be prepared in many ways, from roasting to smoking and even deep-frying. Some key reasons why turkey remains a favorite food include:

  • High in Protein: A lean source of protein, making it a healthy option.
  • Low in Fat: Skinless turkey breast is one of the lowest-fat meats available.
  • Rich in Nutrients: Provides essential vitamins like B6, B12, and niacin, as well as minerals like zinc and selenium.
  • Great for Leftovers: Can be used in sandwiches, soups, casseroles, and even tacos!
